Work Experience

Subway Keyholder

I worked at Subway originally as a Sandwich Artist and my job would be to: make sandwiches, ring up customers, bake bread, clean everything, wash dishes, and other things. After about a few months of doing that, I had got a promotion to Keyholder, and a Keyholder is a person whom holds the key to the store and is in-charge while supervision/management are out of the store. My job as a Keyholder would be supervising everybody to ensure nobody would steal anything and I also had to do counts to make sure the drawer isn't missing a whole bunch of money. I would also have to fill the change machine and count the safe daily. I've had my job for about two years.

Volunteer Work

Lunch Leader

When I attended Hoover Middle School, I had volunteered to be a Lunch Leader. I had started during 7th grade and our job was mainly wiping off tables, moving them to the sides of the room, and last but not least, digging trays out of the garbage that kids would throw. We would also be in-charge of taking out the trash when it got full or sweeping and mopping the floor depending on how bad it was. We always got the cafeteria ready for the janitor to clean.

Office Leader

While I was a Lunch Leader, I had also been volunteering as an Office Leader, during that job I would sit at the front desk and ask people to sign in while also pushing the button to unlock the door into the office when people rang the doorbell. I would also run out recyling and sometimes garbages, or I would have to sort papers and documents in my free time. Usually when a new kid would come to our school or we had elementary children in the building, my job would be to give that person a tour, or if someone's parent had needed to pull them out of school, I would run the pass to their classroom.
